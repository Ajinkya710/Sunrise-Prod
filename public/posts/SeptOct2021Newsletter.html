SUNRISE NEWSLETTER September-October 2021

The past two months brings with it a lot of good news from the SUNRISE team.

On a very positive note, many countries are now seeing an ease in COVID-19 case numbers and as a result, restrictions on movements and activities are beginning to lift. Most importantly, this brings with it opportunities to connect more with those who are closest to us, our friends, and families. Moreover, pertinent to the SUNRISE Study, this allows for the renewal of research activities.

This month Professor Tony Okely has had the pleasure of training three more Phase 3 countries: Mongolia, Chile & Mexico. What an amazing group of professionals committed to improving children’s health. Thank you to all involved, we look forward to working with you and seeing the results of your pilot study!

 

In more good news, Japan and Sweden have both recommenced data collection for the SUNRISE Main Study. Japan began data collection on the 20th of October and have already collected data on more than 20 children, what a terrific way to begin. Sweden has picked up from where they left off before their summer vacation and have successfully assessed over 140 children, and with an incredible compliance rate. We are very lucky to have such fantastic researchers within the SUNRISE Study.

 

Team Morocco have been spotted out and about celebrating the finalisation of the SUNRISE Pilot study. We love to see our teams staying connected, supporting one another, and celebrating the wins – team Morocco, you deserve it!    

The end of 2021 will see an additional three Phase 3 countries trained and commencing data collection, with Ethiopia, Tanzania, and Malawi, in the preparation stages of the pilot study; and if all goes as planned, an additional 11 Phase 3 countries and 3 Main Study countries, will commence before the year ends or in early 2022. We definitely have a busy few months ahead of us!

Finally, I would like to end this update to say a huge congratulations to Najmeh Hamzavi Zarghani from SUNRISE Iran who has recently sat her defence meeting for her PhD!  

 

I hope this month has been kind to you all.

The SUNRISE Coordinating team.
