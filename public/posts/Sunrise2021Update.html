SUNRISE 2021 Update

Firstly, the SUNRISE Coordinating Centre team would like to extend our deepest sympathies, condolences, and our support to our friends within the SUNRISE India team and all nations effected by COVID-19. Our thoughts are with you; wishing you moments of peace and comfort from this terrible humanitarian crisis that is unfolding. Although the world has a long road ahead tackling COVID-19, several countries have seen some reprieve; with the subsequent easing of restrictions allowing training and data collection to recommence. During the beginning of 2021, PNG, Singapore, and Russia have continued to collect data; with both Singapore and Russia just recently completing their data collection for the Phase 3 Pilot Study.    

Sweden, Nigeria, and Morocco have all undergone training and are well underway with their data collection. In the coming months, pilot training is set to take place in Mongolia, Iran, Malawi, and Tanzania – and many more countries are hopeful to partake before the end of the year. We are also excited to welcome researchers from Poland, Kenya and Ethiopia to the SUNRISE study– thank you for joining us! This has brought the total number of countries involved in SUNRISE to over 40. Both Kenya and Ethiopia are looking to undergo training before November 2021. 

 SUNRISE Online Content

As COVID-19 restrictions have limited the UOW Coordinating team’s ability to travel and conduct training face-to-face, the SUNRISE team in Australia have been working on ways to make the training more accessible and to stay connected with our global network, through the use of online content. With the help from our teams in PNG, India, Australia, Chile and the volunteering children and families within these countries, we have created tutorial videos of each assessment. You can access these through our YouTube channel or via the SUNRISE Protocol Manual folder on Cloudstor. The SUNRISE coordinating team also manages a twitter account to showcase the work and experiences of SUNRISE teams around the world.  We encourage you to check this out!

Grant Success

Congratulations to the SUNRISE team in Finland, who were recently granted research funding from the Ministry of Education and Culture to conduct the SUNRISE Main Study!

SUNRISE Publications

On May 17, BMC Public Health published an article by SUNRISE researchers titled: Global effect of COVID-19 pandemic on physical activity, sedentary behaviour and sleep among 3- to 5-year-old children: a longitudinal study of 14 countries. In addition, the SUNRISE team in Bangladesh, led by Mohammad Sorowar Hossain, has submitted an article titled, International Study of 24-hour Movement Behaviors of Early Years (SUNRISE): A Pilot Study from Bangladesh, in BMC Public Health. It is currently under review.
